About Rei

Rei — Rate Engine Intelligence — is a free, data-backed rate calculator built specifically for Philippine-based virtual assistants. It exists because too many skilled VAs are pricing based on guesswork, fear, or outdated advice - and leaving real money on the table every month.

The Problem We're Solving

There's a pricing gap in the Filipino VA industry, and it's not about talent. It's about information.

Thousands of skilled VAs are stuck accepting $3–$5/hr - not because that's what they're worth, but because they've never had a clear, honest framework for figuring out what they should charge. The resources that do exist are either too generic (global freelancing advice that ignores Philippine cost structures), too vague ("charge what you're worth" without defining what that means), or quietly designed to keep rates low so agencies and clients benefit from the gap.

Rei was built to close that gap. One VA at a time, one rate decision at a time.

What Rei Actually Does

Most rate calculators give you a single number based on your niche and call it done. Rei goes deeper. It looks at your real situation:

  • Your monthly expenses in PHP - rent, utilities, food, dependents, the actual cost of your life, not a national average
  • Your target income - what you need to earn to not just survive but build savings, invest in skills, and plan ahead
  • Your available working hours - accounting for the difference between hours worked and hours you can actually bill
  • Your niche and experience level - benchmarked against current market data from OnlineJobs.ph, Upwork, and industry surveys
  • Platform fees and withdrawal costs - the hidden 10–20% that most VAs forget to price around

From those inputs, Rei generates three numbers - not one:

  • A floor rate - the minimum you can charge and still cover your costs. You should never quote below this.
  • A recommended rate - the rate that reflects your skills, experience, and market position. This is where most of your proposals should land.
  • A premium rate - the ceiling for your current profile. This is the rate you grow into as you stack certifications, build your portfolio, and earn client trust.

Three numbers instead of one because pricing isn't a single decision - it's a range you operate within depending on the client, the platform, and the scope of work.

Who This Is For

Rei is for Filipino virtual assistants at every stage who are tired of guessing and ready to price with intention.

If you're just starting out - you need a floor rate before you accept your first client. Without one, you'll say yes to whatever number someone offers, and that number will anchor every rate conversation you have for the next year. Rei gives you a data-backed starting point so your first rate isn't also your lowest.

If you're mid-career and suspect you're undercharging - you probably are. The VAs who raise their rates aren't necessarily more skilled than you. They just have better information about what the market will pay. Rei shows you the gap between where you are and where you could be.

If you're switching niches - your rate resets when your niche changes. A general admin VA moving into bookkeeping or e-commerce needs to recalibrate. Rei benchmarks your new niche so you don't carry an old rate into a higher-value market.

If you're about to ask for a raise - you need a specific number, not a feeling. Rei gives you one you can defend with data. Pair it with the Rate Increase Scripts and the conversation gets a lot easier.

Built by Aleana

Rei was created by Aleana - a Filipino builder, VA, and systems-focused operator who has watched the underpricing problem from the inside for years.

This isn't a tool built from theory. It's built from real experience running VA workflows, real conversations with clients about rates, and real market data tracked over time. Every formula, benchmark, and recommendation inside Rei reflects how the industry actually works - not how blog posts say it should.

The tool is continuously updated using current platform data, freelancer community insights, and rate trends across the most active VA niches. When the market moves, Rei moves with it.

Why This Matters

When a Filipino VA charges $5/hr for work that the market values at $12, the gap doesn't just cost them money. It costs them better clients - because premium clients associate low rates with low quality. It costs them career momentum - because razor-thin margins leave no room to invest in tools, certifications, or time off. And it costs the broader Filipino VA community - because every underpriced proposal pulls the market expectation down for everyone.

Rei exists to push that expectation back up.

Better pricing leads to better clients. Better clients lead to better work. Better work leads to a career you can sustain and grow - not one you're just surviving.

This is bigger than a calculator. It's about raising the standard for Filipino virtual assistants, one rate at a time.
